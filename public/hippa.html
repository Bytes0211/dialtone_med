II Policy Statement

Purpose

This policy defines how ByteStreams LLC complies with HIPAA and HITECH requirements to protect PHI created, received, maintained, or transmitted through the DialTone.Med platform.

Scope

This policy applies to all workforce members, contractors, subcontractors, and business associates handling PHI or ePHI through DialTone.Med systems and processes, including verbal PHI, ePHI, and PHI-derived data retaining identifiers.

III Definitions

  • PHI: Individually identifiable health information in any form, including names, phone numbers, appointment details, DOB, insurance details, and visit reasons captured through phone interactions.
  • ePHI: PHI stored or transmitted electronically, including call recordings, transcriptions, database records, API transmissions, and logs containing identifiers.
  • Covered Entity: Healthcare providers and organizations subject to HIPAA; DialTone.Med client practices are Covered Entities.
  • Business Associate: A person/entity performing services involving PHI on behalf of a Covered Entity; ByteStreams LLC operates in this role.
  • Minimum Necessary: Limit uses/disclosures of PHI to only what is reasonably required for the intended purpose.
  • Workforce Member: Any person under organizational control performing work, paid or unpaid.
  • Security Incident: Attempted/successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, destruction, or interference involving information systems.
  • Breach: Impermissible access/use/disclosure of PHI presumed to be a breach unless low-probability compromise is demonstrated under the four-factor test in 45 CFR 164.402.

IV Roles and Responsibilities

HIPAA Compliance Officer

  • Oversee HIPAA Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement compliance
  • Maintain policies/procedures and monitor regulatory updates
  • Conduct/document risk assessments, audits, and compliance records
  • Manage breach detection, investigation, response, and required notifications
  • Approve BAAs and coordinate HIPAA matters with legal counsel as needed

IT / Technical Operations

  • Implement encryption, access controls, and audit logging
  • Monitor alerts/logs and maintain secure system configurations
  • Manage vulnerability assessment, incident response, patching, backup, DR, and business continuity
  • Maintain secure software development practices for the DialTone.Med platform

All Workforce Members and Contractors

  • Comply with HIPAA policies and complete required training
  • Report suspected incidents/breaches immediately
  • Access only minimum necessary PHI
  • Protect credentials, secure devices, and cooperate with audits

V Privacy Procedures

Minimum Necessary Standard

DialTone.Med workflows are designed to collect and process only PHI needed for the requested function. Access to PHI is role-based and reviewed quarterly.

Authorization

Any use/disclosure not permitted by HIPAA Privacy Rule requires valid written authorization including PHI scope, authorized discloser/recipient, purpose, expiration, signature/date, and revocation rights.

Patient Rights Support

  • Right of Access
  • Right to Amendment
  • Right to Restriction
  • Right to Confidential Communications
  • Right to Accounting of Disclosures
  • Right to File Complaints without retaliation

VI Security Procedures

Administrative Safeguards

  • Annual risk analysis and risk management plan updates
  • Role-based and least-privilege access management
  • Incident handling with containment, investigation, remediation, and post-incident review
  • Contingency planning (daily encrypted backups, DR, emergency operations, annual testing)
  • Annual security evaluation and documented control improvements

Physical Safeguards

  • Restricted facility access and cloud data center physical controls
  • Workstation protections (auto-lock, full-disk encryption, secured remote access)
  • Device/media disposal and sanitization procedures with accountability logging

Technical Safeguards

  • Unique user IDs, MFA, automatic logoff, and emergency access procedures
  • AES-256 at-rest encryption and TLS 1.2+ in-transit encryption
  • Comprehensive audit logging retained for at least six years
  • Integrity controls (checksums/versioning/alerts)
  • Transmission protections including encrypted API and telephony channels

VII Training and Awareness

  • Initial HIPAA training within 30 days and before PHI system access
  • Annual refresher training and event-driven supplemental training
  • Training records retained for minimum six years
  • Topics include Privacy Rule, Security Rule, incident reporting, sanctions, and role-specific procedures

VIII Breach Notification

  • Suspected/confirmed breaches must be reported immediately and within 24 hours to the Compliance Officer
  • Risk assessment uses four HIPAA factors under 45 CFR 164.402
  • Individual notices: without unreasonable delay, no later than 60 days
  • HHS notices: 500+ individuals within 60 days; fewer than 500 annually within 60 days of year-end
  • Media notices: required for breaches affecting 500+ residents of one jurisdiction
  • Covered Entity notices: as Business Associate, notify affected clients without unreasonable delay and within 30 days
  • Breach documentation retained for minimum six years with mitigation/corrective action records

IX Sanctions

Violations may result in sanctions up to and including contract termination and referral for prosecution when warranted. Sanctions are documented and proportional to severity, intent, harm, training history, and prior violations.

Good-faith reporting is mandatory and protected under a strict non-retaliation policy.

X Documentation and Retention

HIPAA-related documentation is maintained in written electronic form and retained for at least six years from creation or last effective date, whichever is later.

XI Assumptions

  • ByteStreams LLC is committed to HIPAA/HITECH and applicable Omnibus Rule obligations.
  • State laws more stringent than HIPAA are followed where applicable.
  • Training, risk management, and incident response are ongoing operational requirements.
  • BAAs are required with all relevant subcontractors and business associates.
  • Continuous privacy/security improvement and complete compliance documentation are core requirements.
  • No retaliation is permitted for good-faith compliance reporting.

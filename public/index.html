The Problem

Missing calls is a bigger problem...

Frustrated patients and lost revenue

Key Points

  • Phones are infinite, humans are finite front desk staff are checking patients in out while the phone rings prettygoodai.com
  • Peak hours are the worst Monday mornings, lunch, and end-of-day see the highest miss rates, exactly when patients are most likely to call
  • Voicemail is a dead end 75-85% of callers who hit voicemail vanish forever
  • No one knows it's happening there's no "missed revenue" line item on any P&L vigma.ai

Practice Data Points

  • Average Miss Rate

    30-42% of calls are missed during business hours.

  • Landmark Study

    7,000 calls across 22 practices in 18 states showed 42% missed calls.

  • Patient Behavior

    60% hang up on hold, and 30% of those never call back.

  • Voicemail Drop-off

    80% who reach voicemail do not leave a message.

  • Revenue Risk

    Annual impact is typically $200K-$500K, and can exceed $1M in high-volume specialties.

  • After-hours Demand

    About 40% of patient calls happen outside standard business hours.

  • Average Miss Rate

    28-38% of inbound calls are missed.

  • Peerlogic Study

    4,280 calls across 26 practices found 38% unanswered during business hours.

  • Monthly Missed Calls

    Roughly 300 calls are missed per average practice each month.

  • Voicemail Abandonment

    78-85% of callers who hit voicemail never call back.

  • Competitor Defection

    67% who cannot get through immediately call another dentist.

  • Revenue and LTV Risk

    A mid-size practice can lose $100K-$150K+ annually; one missed new patient may cost roughly $5,400 in lifetime value.
